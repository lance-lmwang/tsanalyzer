# PCR Jitter Root Cause Engine

The PCR Jitter Root Cause Engine determines whether timing instability originates from the **encoder** or the **network transport path**. While traditional analyzers only report a single jitter value, TsAnalyzer identifies the **origin of the jitter** using independent differential measurements.

---

## 1. Core Diagnostic Principle

The engine correlates two independent delta measurements:

1.  **PCR Value Delta ($\Delta V$)**: The timestamp increment generated by the encoder's clock.
2.  **PCR Arrival Delta ($\Delta A$)**: The real-world arrival time of the packet as measured by the hardware clock (HAT).

| Metric | Source | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PCR Value** | Encoder | Reflects the multiplexer's internal timing intent. |
| **PCR Arrival**| Network | Reflects the physical delivery consistency. |

---

## 2. Measurement Model

$$\Delta V = PCR[n] - PCR[n-1]$$
$$\Delta A = HAT[n] - HAT[n-1]$$

In an ideal system, $\Delta V \approx \Delta A$. Deviations are classified as follows:

### 2.1 Case: Encoder Jitter (Clock Instability)
*   **Observation**: $\Delta V$ is irregular/unstable, but $\Delta A$ is constant.
*   **Diagnosis**: The encoder's internal clock or multiplexer scheduler is unstable.
*   **Evidence**: PCR spacing in the bitstream is malformed despite perfect network delivery.

### 2.2 Case: Network Jitter (Transport Impairment)
*   **Observation**: $\Delta V$ is stable, but $\Delta A$ is irregular.
*   **Diagnosis**: Network congestion, switch queuing delay, or SRT retransmission.
*   **Evidence**: The encoder sent a perfect clock, but the network scrambled the arrival times.

### 2.3 Case: Mux Overload / Congestion
*   **Observation**: Both $\Delta V$ and $\Delta A$ are unstable.
*   **Diagnosis**: Multiplex buffer overflow or severe network burst shaping.

---

## 3. Output Metrology & Confidence

The engine outputs a root cause classification with an associated confidence score based on the variance correlation coefficient.

**Example Output**:
*   **PCR Jitter**: 480 ns
*   **Root Cause**: `NETWORK_JITTER`
*   **Confidence**: 92%
*   **$\Delta V$ Variance**: 0.02
*   **$\Delta A$ Variance**: 0.85
