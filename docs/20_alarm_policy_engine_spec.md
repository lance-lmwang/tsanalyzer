# Technical Spec: Alarm Policy & Escalation Engine (Layer 5)

The Alarm Policy & Escalation Engine (Layer 5) applies business rules to the stateful alarms generated by Layer 4. It handles suppression, multi-indicator escalation, and maintenance windows to reduce alert fatigue and focus on critical issues.

---

## 1. Multi-Indicator Policy Evaluation

The engine evaluates complex rules that correlate multiple engineering indicators to determine the final severity of an alarm.

### 1.1 Escalation Rules (Example)
Policies are defined as "If-Then" logic combining Layer 4 states and Layer 3/6 metrics.

| Rule ID | Condition | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **POL_01** | `TR_101_290_P1_1_Active` AND `MDI_DF_Rolling_60s > 20ms` | Escalate to **CRITICAL** (Red) |
| **POL_02** | `CC_Error_Count_1m > 10` AND `PCR_Jitter_Max > 1000ns` | Escalate to **MAJOR** (Orange) |
| **POL_03** | `SRT_Retransmit_Rate > 15%` AND `SLA_Availability_24h < 99.9%` | Escalate to **MAJOR** |

---

## 2. Alarm Suppression & Maintenance

To ensure "Operational Silence" during planned events, the engine implements two suppression mechanisms.

### 2.1 Maintenance Windows
- **Definition**: A timed window (start/end) where specific streams or probes are excluded from active alerting.
- **Behavior**: Alarms entering the **ACTIVE** state during this window are marked as `SUPPRESSED`. They are logged for history but do not trigger notifications or impact real-time Risk KPIs.

### 2.2 Dependency Suppression (Root Cause Silence)
- **Logic**: If a "Root" alarm is active, "Child" alarms are suppressed.
- **Example**: If `Ingress_Link_Down` is active, suppress all downstream `PAT_Error`, `PMT_Error`, and `CC_Error` alarms for that stream. This prevents the "Alarm Storm" common in network failures.

---

## 3. Alarm Correlation & Merging (Burst Management)

The engine groups rapid, repeating errors into a single logical **Incident**.

- **Correlation Window**: Default `60 seconds`.
- **Logic**: If 50 CC errors occur on the same PID within 60s, the system generates **ONE Incident** with a count of 50, rather than 50 individual alarms.
- **State Link**: The Incident only closes when the last correlated error has been `CLEARED` for the `T_clear` duration.

## 5. Integration Flow

1.  **FSM Event**: Layer 4 reports a state transition (e.g., `OPEN` to `ACTIVE`).
2.  **Suppression Check**: Layer 5 checks the Maintenance Database. If suppressed, flag the alarm and stop.
3.  **Policy Engine**: Evaluate escalation rules against the current `tsa_history_t` (Layer 6) and `tsa_kpi_t` (Layer 7).
4.  **Route & Dispatch**: If escalation occurs, update the alarm object's `severity` and dispatch to Layer 8 (Presentation/API).
5.  **History Link**: All policy decisions (Suppressed/Escalated) are logged in Layer 6 for compliance auditing.
